Common European Framework of Reference:

| Articles

“A” is used before a consonant sound

I have a dog and you have a cat.

You have a cat and I have a parrot.

“An” is used before a vowel sound

I have an apple.

You have an iPhone.

We have an apartment.

| Verb To Be

- Affirmative

I am brazilian.

I'm a teacher.

You are a doctor.

We are students.

They're happy.

- Negative

I am not brazilian.

I'm not a teacher.

You are not a doctor.

We aren't students.

They're not happy.

- Interrogative

Am I brazilian?

Am I a teacher?

Are you a doctor?

Are we students?

Are they happy?
